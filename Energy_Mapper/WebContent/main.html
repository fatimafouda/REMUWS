It is speculated by experts that by the end of the decade, non-renewable energy resources like oil and gas will no longer be sufficient to fulfill global consumption needs. As the Earthâ€™s fossil fuel reserves continue to diminish, governments are exerting efforts to search for more ways to either partially or fully transition to renewable energy resources instead. One of the major factors involved in maximizing the efficiency of these renewable energy resources throughout the year is location; that is, where is the optimum location for building a solar plant, or a wind farm. Given also Egypt's 90+% of desert areas and growing population, the need for relocation to new areas supported by energy is also in demand. The proposed project aims to utilize a network of sensors placed in very remote areas to record necessary information on wind and solar light. The obtained information is then processed to obtain statistics and energy output forecasts that can be placed on a map for strategic decision on making on where to deploy energy farms.
